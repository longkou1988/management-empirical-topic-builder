# Analysis Rules

## Evidence Discipline

- Every literature fact must come from Zotero metadata, PDF text, abstract, notes, annotations, or an approved fallback source.
- Do not infer a DOI, journal, variable, theory, method, sample, result, or contribution that is not present in the source.
- Use `不足以判断` when a field cannot be supported.
- Use `仅基于摘要分析` when only abstract or metadata is available.
- Use `全文不可用` when PDF full text is missing, unreadable, incomplete, or garbled.
- Evidence text should be short and specific. Prefer paraphrase in synthesis.

## Literature Type Classification

Classify from explicit evidence in abstract, method, data, sample, analysis, findings, results, or discussion.

- Quantitative research: statistical testing, survey, archival data, experiment, panel data, regression, SEM, PLS-SEM, CB-SEM, PROCESS, HLM, fsQCA, NCA, or similar empirical model testing.
- Qualitative research: case study, interviews, ethnography, grounded theory, Gioia method, thematic coding, process tracing, qualitative comparative field evidence.
- Mixed methods: meaningful combination of quantitative and qualitative evidence or sequential/parallel mixed design.
- Theory paper: conceptual model or theory development without original empirical testing.
- Review paper: structured synthesis of a literature stream, including systematic review or narrative review.
- Method paper: primary contribution is measurement, design, identification, or analytical method.
- Meta-analysis paper: statistical synthesis of effect sizes across prior studies.

Confidence:

- `High`: direct method/results evidence or full-text section evidence.
- `Medium`: abstract and metadata clearly indicate design, but full details are limited.
- `Low`: only indirect clues exist or source access is partial.

## Variable Role Rules

- Extract variable roles only when the paper supports them through a model figure, hypotheses, measurement section, results table, or discussion.
- A construct can appear in multiple roles across papers; keep separate rows by paper and role.
- Distinguish construct level when possible: individual, team, organization, interorganizational, platform, industry, country, or second-order construct.
- For paths, record whether the path was hypothesized, tested, significant, non-significant, mixed, or opposite in direction.
- If a role appears plausible but is not stated or testable from the text, mark `不足以判断` instead of assigning a role.

## Qualitative Mechanism Rules

- Separate data structure levels: first-order concepts, second-order themes, aggregate dimensions, and process mechanisms.
- Do not convert qualitative themes into quantitative variables unless the paper supplies enough conceptual clarity.
- Boundary conditions must be grounded in the paper's setting, cases, contrasts, or limitations.

## Theory Gap Rules

Identify these gap types:

1. Theory gap: existing theory does not explain a phenomenon, relationship, level, or context well.
2. Mechanism gap: prior studies document relationships but under-explain the process.
3. Boundary condition gap: missing moderator, scope condition, or contextual contingency.
4. Context gap: concentration in specific countries, industries, groups, platforms, or institutional environments.
5. Method gap: overreliance on cross-sectional surveys, single-source data, one method, or weak identification.
6. Conflicting result: inconsistent significance or direction for a comparable relationship.
7. Variable role gap: a construct is usually treated as outcome but can be theorized as antecedent, mediator, or moderator.
8. Practical problem gap: current studies do not explain a new or salient management phenomenon.

A gap is usable only when supported by multiple papers or by a clear limitation/future research statement. Mark single-paper gaps as exploratory.

## Variable Network and Model Recombination

Build the network from observed variable-role rows. Summarize frequencies by role and map recurring paths. Then identify:

- High-frequency IVs, DVs, mediators, and moderators.
- Overused variables that may have low novelty unless reframed.
- Variables with role-repositioning potential.
- Rare combinations with a plausible shared theory, mechanism, level, and context.

A model candidate must satisfy all conditions:

- It answers a clear management question.
- It has an antecedent-mechanism-outcome-boundary structure or a defensible alternative.
- Every construct has a theoretical reason to enter the model.
- The research object and empirical context are specified.
- The method and data source are plausible.
- Publication risk is stated.
- The explanation says why the model is not simple variable拼接.

## Topic Generation Rules

Generate at least 10 topics only after the matrices and gaps are built. Each topic must be grounded in the corpus evidence but can propose a new combination when the theoretical bridge is explicit.

For every topic, include:

- One clear research question.
- Theory story chain: phenomenon -> theory tension -> antecedent -> mechanism -> outcome -> boundary condition -> contribution.
- Hypotheses that match the model structure.
- Data and method plan appropriate to the variables and context.
- Innovation point and publication risk.
- Suitable management journal direction, such as organizational behavior, human resource management, strategy, entrepreneurship, innovation, information systems, operations, or international business.

## Reviewer Evaluation Rules

Score each topic from an SSCI management reviewer perspective:

- Theory contribution: Does it extend, challenge, integrate, or contextualize theory?
- Practical significance: Does it address a concrete management problem?
- Model clarity: Is the causal story coherent and parsimonious?
- Method feasibility: Can the model be tested credibly?
- Data availability: Is the proposed data realistically obtainable?
- Novelty: Is the topic meaningfully different from the corpus?

Publication risk:

- `低`: clear contribution, feasible data, coherent model, manageable novelty risk.
- `中`: promising but has theory, measurement, or identification risk.
- `高`: weak theory novelty, hard data access, unclear causality, or likely reviewer skepticism.

Overall recommendation:

- `强烈推荐`: top-tier potential and feasible execution.
- `推荐`: solid paper potential with manageable risks.
- `谨慎推荐`: usable but needs narrowing or stronger evidence.
- `不推荐`: weak contribution or infeasible design.

## Introduction and Hypotheses Next Steps

For top topics, recommend how to deepen into a full paper:

1. Convert the practical problem into a theory puzzle.
2. Define the focal phenomenon and boundary of the literature conversation.
3. Use the corpus gaps to craft the introduction's problematization.
4. Turn the model story chain into hypotheses in causal order.
5. Match measurement and data collection to each construct level.
6. Add identification, endogeneity, common-method, and robustness plans where relevant.
